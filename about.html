About Project

This project is intended to predict when the highest number of each species starts to arrive in each region of Ontario. The methodology based on Mike Burrell's blog post. Instead of using eBird data from every available year, only the past 20 years were used to have a more accurate prediction based on current circumstances.

A more detailed explanation of the project will be published soon.

To expand on the idea, I will be calculating the average "mass arrival" dates for each species as they change year to year, and how accurate each year turned out to be. This should highlight the effects of climate change - showing exactly how each species' migration timing has altered.

All bird data is from eBird.

eBird. 2021. eBird: An online database of bird distribution and abundance [web application]. eBird, Cornell Lab of Ornithology, Ithaca, New York. Available: http://www.ebird.org. (Accessed: April 5 & June 9 2022).
